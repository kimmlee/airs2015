Student Travel Support

AIRS offers small grants to students presenting at the conference to help cover the cost of travel, lodging and registration. To be elegible for travel support, students must be authors of full, short or demo papers and presenters of the work at the conference. Student authors of full, short and demo papers who do not present the work at the conference are not eligible for these travel grants.

In most cases, the travel grants will only cover a portion of the travel expenses. Our goal is to maximize the number of students we can help. If the demand exceeds the available budget, we will prioritize applicants who are full-time students at the time of the conference and those from low-income countries. These grants are given in the form of reimbursement, so students will need to plan to cover their costs upfront. The awardees will only receive money after producing receipts for appropriate expenses, and completing any supplied forms. The reimbursement will be processed through the ACM account. Note that the organising committees reserve the right to award more or less grants as they see fit, or to alter the amount of any grants.

Students who receive travel grants are expected to register using the “early-bird” conference registration rates and to travel and lodge as economically as possible.

How to apply

Send an email to the student travel support chair of the conference (Teerapong Leelanupab, airs2015.grant@gmail.com) with the subject line including all accpeted paper IDs separeated by comma, such as "AIRS 2015 Student Travel Support Request - 100, 321". The email must be cc'ed to your supervisor. In the email, we want all applicants to give detials as follows:

  1. Be sure to include name, affiliation, country, type of student (undergraduate/honours, masters, phd).
  2. If you have one or more paper at AIRS; if yes, then titles and list of authors of each paper.
  3. Where will you travel from?
  4. Explain briefly why you should get the travel grant.
  5. What is the budget for your trip: you must breakdown all your expenses?
  6. What support do you receive from your university, i.e., how much is your university paying of the total budget (allow also for unknown)?
  7. How much amount do you expect to receive from AIRS (we have a very tight budget; so, please faithfully give us true information)?
  8. The name and email of your supervisor for confirming student status and budget.
You must also arrange for your supervisor to send a separate email acknowledging that you are indeed enrolled as a student, and that the statements you made in the application are correct (to the best of their knowledge). An example of subject line for a supervisor's email with all accpeted paper IDs separeated by comma is "Supervisor's Acknowledgement for AIRS 2015 Student Travel Support Request - 100, 321".
